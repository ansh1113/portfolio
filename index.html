My Vision

The Humanoid as a Platform

The robotics industry is at a turning point. It can continue building single-purpose tools, the digital calculators and the cameras, in a world that is ready for the smartphone, or it can forge a new path. My vision is to create that path by building the robotic "smartphone": a universal platform designed to master any skill imaginable.


This work is anchored by my guiding principle: "One Robot. Many lives." It’s not just about creating a machine, but a new paradigm for human-robot collaboration that can adapt to any environment and elevate any life it touches. I envision this platform becoming a seamless part of our world: in our homes, our offices, and our hospitals; managing warehouses, patrolling streets, and leading the way in disaster response.


This is achieved through a unique architecture:


The foundation is the Universal Chassis - a single, general-purpose hardware body, engineered for an unparalleled range of motion and dexterity. This core body is then augmented by a system of modular attachments, allowing it to physically specialize for certain domains, from delicate medical sensors to heavy-duty industrial grippers, without compromising its fundamental generality.


The robot's true, limitless capability comes from its Skill Ecosystem. Like an app store for robotics, this is an ever-expanding library of mastered abilities. Each skill, from complex logistical analysis to delicate domestic tasks, is a perfected, verified block of capability.


The "magic" is the Core Intelligence. This AI is the robot's operating system. It translates human intent into flawless execution. When given a complex command, it instantly orchestrates a novel sequence of skills from its library to compose the perfect solution, a symphony of mastered actions.


This architecture is the key to achieving the impossible: a robot that is both a generalist and a master. It sidesteps the "jack-of-all-trades" paradox because its generality arises from its ability to run thousands of specialized, mastered "skill-apps" to perfection.


Crucially, this entire system is built on a non-negotiable foundation of safety and trust. The user is always in command, with absolute authority through a secure control app. This authority is made tangible through innovative features like a "simulation-first" protocol: before any complex new action, the robot runs a virtual test of its plan, which the user can view and approve. Only with explicit consent does the robot act in the real world.


This isn't just about building the next step in automation. It's about building a partner for humanity.

Featured Projects

Ideas Lab

A few of my favorite “next big thing” robotics ideas—let’s build the future together.

Wall & Roof Climbing Robot

A modular robot platform that can adhere to and navigate vertical or inverted surfaces - walls, ceilings, pipelines; using bio-inspired suction, SLAM, and intelligent path planning. Potential use cases: industrial inspection, disaster zone reconnaissance, and confined space exploration.

Adaptive Vision-Based Sorting Robot

Conveyor-side robot with real time object detection and dynamic grip control. Designed for high throughput sorting in recycling centers, warehouses, or agri-processing plants. Think a fast-learning eye + hand combo for unstructured materials.

Human-Aware Robotic Wheelchair

An assistive mobility platform with autonomous indoor navigation, obstacle anticipation (not just avoidance), and gesture + voice control. Built for intuitive collaboration and trust with the user, especially in eldercare and rehabilitation settings.

Solar-Driven Autonomous Field Rover

Long duration rover designed for agriculture, perimeter monitoring, or environmental mapping. Uses solar charging, power aware mission planning, and terrain adaptive locomotion to operate in low infrastructure zones.

Multi-Modal Service Robot for Human Interaction

A personal robotic assistant that interprets voice, gestures, and emotional cues to assist in home, lab, or healthcare environments. Prioritizes natural interaction through sensor fusion and learning-based intent recognition.

Drone Escort for Cyclist Safety

A lightweight drone that maps traffic and gives cyclists dynamic safety alerts and optimized route suggestions in real time. Geofenced and designed for short bursts of micro-navigation in urban setups.

Quadruped for Terrain-Aware Disaster Response

A low cost, semi autonomous quadruped that can navigate rubble, stairs, or tight corridors during search-and-rescue ops. Powered by onboard vision + proprioception, reinforcement learning for adaptive gaiting, and low latency teleoperation fallback.

Edge-Compute Swarm Drones for GPS-Denied Zones

A fleet of compact drones equipped with onboard compute, decentralized SLAM, and vision-based coordination for search missions in GPS-denied areas like forests, tunnels, or caves. Inspired by your multi-UAV swarm and RL work.
